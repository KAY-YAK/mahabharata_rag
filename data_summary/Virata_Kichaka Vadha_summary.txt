Summary of Kichaka Vadha parva (under Virata parva)

**Summary**

The story revolves around Droupadi, a princess who lives in disguise as Sairandhri in the city of Matsya with her husbands, the Pandavas. She faces unhappiness and miseries due to their situation, where they are forced to live in hiding and Yudhishthira's addiction to gambling has led to their downfall. Droupadi expresses her grief and suffering to Bhima, who promises to kill Kichaka, Virata's general who has been molesting her.

Droupadi fixes an assignation with Kichaka in the dancing hall, where Bhima kills him in secret. The sutas, Kichaka's relatives, try to burn Droupadi alive as revenge, but Bhima rescues her and kills many of them. Droupadi is freed from danger and returns to the city, where she is welcomed back by the people.

**Key events and characters**

- Droupadi lives in disguise as Sairandhri in the city of Matsya with her husbands.
- Kichaka, Virata's general, molests Droupadi repeatedly.
- Bhima promises to kill Kichaka on Droupadi's behalf.
- Droupadi fixes an assignation with Kichaka in the dancing hall.
- Bhima kills Kichaka in secret.
- The sutas try to burn Droupadi alive as revenge.
- Bhima rescues Droupadi and kills many of the sutas.
- Droupadi returns to the city, where she is welcomed back by the people.

**Philosophical or Moral Ideas**

- The importance of protecting one's wife and adhering to dharma (righteous living).
- The consequences of addiction and the loss of self-control.
- The power of destiny and the inevitability of fate.
- The need for women to be protected and respected in society.
