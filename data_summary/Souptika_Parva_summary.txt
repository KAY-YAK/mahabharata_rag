Summary of Souptika parva. This is the summary of main parva, Souptika parva.

**Summary**

The story continues with Sanjaya describing events after Duryodhana's death. Ashvatthama, Kripa, and Kritavarma flee but are overcome by grief and seek revenge on the Pandavas while they sleep. They encounter a powerful being guarding the camp gate, which forces them to retreat. Ashvatthama seeks refuge with Shiva, offering himself as a sacrifice. Shiva gives him a powerful sword and enters his body. Ashvatthama kills Dhrishtadyumna and continues to slaughter the Pandavas and their allies. The three warriors then go to where Duryodhana lies dying.

Dhritarashtra is informed of the events, and he becomes immersed in deep thoughts. Later, Yudhishthira learns about the death of his sons and brothers. Ashvatthama kills thousands of men, elephants, and horses while they are asleep. Krishna (Droupadi) is brought to Yudhishthira and is overcome with grief. Bhimasena sets out on his chariot to find Ashvatthama, accompanied by Arjuna and Yudhishthira.

**Key events and characters**

- Ashvatthama seeks revenge on the Pandavas while they sleep.
- He encounters a powerful being guarding the camp gate.
- Ashvatthama kills Dhrishtadyumna and continues to slaughter the Pandavas and their allies.
- Dhritarashtra becomes immersed in deep thoughts upon learning of the events.
- Yudhishthira learns about the death of his sons and brothers.
- Ashvatthama kills thousands of men, elephants, and horses while they are asleep.
- Bhimasena sets out on his chariot to find Ashvatthama.

**Philosophical or Moral Ideas**

- The concept of destiny (dharma) is emphasized throughout the text.
- Revenge can lead to chaos and destruction.
- The importance of following one's dharma, even if it means facing adversity.
