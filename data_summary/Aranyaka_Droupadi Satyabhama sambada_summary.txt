Summary of Droupadi Satyabhama sambada parva (under Aranyaka parva)

**Summary**

The text describes a conversation between Satyabhama, the queen of Krishna, and Droupadi, the wife of Pandavas. Satyabhama asks Droupadi about her secret to controlling her husbands, but Droupadi refuses to reveal it, saying that she does not follow evil practices like using mantras or roots to control men. Instead, she explains that she serves her husbands with humility and devotion, following their instructions and taking care of their needs.

Droupadi also shares how she manages the household, including feeding brahmanas and supporting various servants and ascetics. She emphasizes the importance of serving one's husband with love and affection, and warns Satyabhama about the dangers of being friends with evil women or spending time alone with her sons.

Satyabhama is impressed by Droupadi's words and asks for her forgiveness for speaking out of turn. Droupadi then shares a secret way to capture the heart of one's husband, which involves worshiping him with love and affectionate deeds.

The text ends with Satyabhama taking her leave and praising Droupadi's conduct.

**Key events and characters**

- Satyabhama asks Droupadi about her secret to controlling her husbands.
- Droupadi refuses to reveal it, saying she does not follow evil practices.
- Droupadi explains how she serves her husbands with humility and devotion.
- She shares how she manages the household, including feeding brahmanas and supporting servants and ascetics.
- Satyabhama is impressed by Droupadi's words and asks for her forgiveness.
- Droupadi shares a secret way to capture the heart of one's husband.
- Satyabhama takes her leave and praises Droupadi's conduct.

**Philosophical or Moral Ideas**

- The importance of serving one's husband with love and affection.
- The dangers of being friends with evil women or spending time alone with sons.
- The need to follow a virtuous path in order to achieve happiness.
- The idea that a woman should be dependent on her husband and respect his authority.
- The emphasis on humility, devotion, and self-control as essential qualities for a virtuous woman.
