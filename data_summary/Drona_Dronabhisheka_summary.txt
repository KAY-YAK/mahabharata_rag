Summary of Dronabhisheka parva (under Drona parva)

**Summary**:
The battle between the Pandavas and their allies against the Kauravas is intense and chaotic. Drona, a skilled warrior, roams amidst the Pandavas, burning down soldiers with his arrows like a fierce fire. The battle is compared to a river with various creatures, symbolizing chaos and destruction.

Individual battles between specific warriors are described in vivid detail, including Abhimanyu fighting Pourava, Jayadratha fighting Subhadra's son, and Shalya attacking Subhadra's son with a javelin made of iron and decorated with gold. The bravery and skill of individual warriors are highlighted, but also their vulnerability to injury and death.

The battle rages on, with various warriors clashing in fierce duels. Drona is eventually repulsed by Kumara, who pierces him in the chest with an arrow. However, Drona overcomes his fatigue and continues to fight, slaying many warriors, including Kumara.

As the battle reaches its climax, Yudhishthira's army begins to slaughter the enemy soldiers, driving the maharathas away. Drona is eventually killed by Kunti's son, who creates a great darkness with his arrows and shrouds the field of battle.

**Key events and characters**
- Drona roams amidst the Pandavas, burning down soldiers with his arrows.
- The battle is compared to a river with various creatures.
- Abhimanyu fights Pourava, using a sword and shield to defend himself.
- Jayadratha fights Subhadra's son, using a sword and shield in a fierce duel.
- Shalya attacks Subhadra's son with a javelin made of iron and decorated with gold.
- Drona is repulsed by Kumara, who pierces him in the chest with an arrow.
- Kunti's son kills Drona with his arrows.

**Philosophical or Moral Ideas**
- The text describes the chaos and destruction caused by war, comparing it to a river with various creatures.
- The bravery and skill of individual warriors are highlighted, but also their vulnerability to injury and death.
