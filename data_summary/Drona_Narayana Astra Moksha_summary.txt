Summary of Narayana Astra Moksha parva (under Drona parva)

**Summary**

Dhritarashtra asks Sanjaya about the battle where Drona, a great warrior, was killed. Sanjaya tells Dhritarashtra that Arjuna saw a great wonder after killing Drona and asked Vyasa who this being was. Vyasa explained that the being is Shankara, also known as Mahadeva or Shiva, the lord of all energy and the god of the earth, sky, and heaven. Vyasa described Shiva in many different ways, listing his various forms, names, and attributes.

**Key events and characters**

- Dhritarashtra asks Sanjaya about the battle where Drona was killed.
- Arjuna sees a great wonder that signified his victory after killing Drona.
- Arjuna asks Vyasa who this being is.
- Vyasa explains that the being is Shankara, also known as Mahadeva or Shiva.
- Sanjaya tells Dhritarashtra about Vyasa's explanation of Shiva.

**Philosophical or Moral Ideas**

- The importance of seeking refuge with a higher power, such as Shiva.
- The idea that the universe is pervaded by a divine energy or force, which is embodied in Shiva.
- The concept of multiple forms and names for a single deity, highlighting the complexity and diversity of the divine.
- The emphasis on the worship and reverence due to Shiva, who grants boons and objects of desire to men.
