Summary of Ratha Atiratha Samkhya parva (under Udyoga parva)

**Summary**

The text describes a conversation between Dhritarashtra and Sanjaya about the upcoming war. Sanjaya tells Dhritarashtra that Bhishma has become the commander of the Kouravas and has spoken to Duryodhana, assuring him that he will protect his army and defeat the Pandavas. Bhishma lists the names of various rathas (warriors) on both sides, including their strengths and weaknesses. He describes some as maharathas (mighty warriors), atirathas (unrivalled warriors), or half-rathas (those who are not fully skilled). The conversation highlights the military preparations and strategies of both armies.

**Key events and characters**

- Bhishma becomes the commander of the Kouravas.
- Duryodhana is assured by Bhishma that he will protect his army and defeat the Pandavas.
- Bhishma lists various rathas on both sides, including their strengths and weaknesses.
- The conversation highlights the military preparations and strategies of both armies.

**Philosophical or Moral Ideas**

- The importance of listening to the aged but also not being swayed by their opinions if they are deluded (Bhishma's speech).
- The idea that a kshatriya is known for his strength, while a brahmana is known for his mantras and a vaishya for his riches (Karna's speech).
- The concept of dharma (righteousness) and the importance of following it as a kshatriya (Bhishma's speech).
