Summary of Karna parva. This is the summary of main parva, Karna parva.

**Summary**

The scene is set after Karna's death in a battle between the Pandavas and the Kurus. Sanjaya narrates to Duryodhana about the destruction caused by Arjuna's arrows. Many soldiers, horses, and elephants are killed or injured on the battlefield. Shalya tries to console Duryodhana but he is overcome with grief. The king of Madra describes the battlefield as a scene of devastation. Sanjaya praises Arjuna and Krishna for their bravery in the battle. Yudhishthira praises Krishna and Arjuna for their victory, saying it's due to good fortune. Dhritarashtra falls unconscious after hearing the news of Karna's death.

**Key events and characters**

- Karna is killed by Arjuna in battle.
- The Kurus are defeated and their army is destroyed.
- Shalya tries to console Duryodhana after Karna's death.
- Many soldiers, horses, and elephants are killed or injured on the battlefield.
- Sanjaya narrates the scene to Duryodhana.
- Arjuna and Krishna (also known as Janardana) are praised for their bravery.
- Yudhishthira is informed about Karna's death by Krishna.
- Dhritarashtra falls unconscious after hearing the news of Karna's death.

**Philosophical or Moral Ideas**

- Destiny is a natural force that cannot be changed ("Destiny flows according to its own rules").
- The virtuous and righteous should be respected and admired (Karna's generosity and love for giving are highlighted).
