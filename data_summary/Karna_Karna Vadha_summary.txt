Summary of Karna Vadha parva (under Karna parva)

**Summary**

The scene is set after Karna's death in a battle between the Pandavas and the Kurus. Sanjaya narrates to Duryodhana about the destruction caused by Arjuna's arrows, which killed many soldiers, horses, and elephants. Shalya tries to console Duryodhana but he is overcome with grief. The king of Madra describes the battlefield as a scene of devastation where many warriors have been killed or injured. Despite his efforts to comfort Duryodhana, the king's mind remains filled with sorrow.

Sanjaya praises Arjuna and Krishna for their bravery in the battle. He says that Karna was a great warrior who gave everything he possessed to brahmanas but was ultimately killed by Arjuna. The scene shifts to Yudhishthira, who is informed about Karna's death by Krishna. Yudhishthira praises Krishna and Arjuna for their victory and says that it is due to good fortune.

The scene ends with Dhritarashtra, the king of the Kurus, falling unconscious after hearing the news of Karna's death.

**Key events and characters**

- Karna is killed by Arjuna in battle.
- The Kurus are defeated and their army is destroyed.
- Shalya tries to console Duryodhana after Karna's death.
- Many soldiers, horses, and elephants are killed or injured on the battlefield.
- Sanjaya narrates the scene to Duryodhana.
- Arjuna and Krishna (also known as Janardana) are praised for their bravery.
- Yudhishthira is informed about Karna's death by Krishna.
- Dhritarashtra falls unconscious after hearing the news of Karna's death.

**Philosophical or Moral Ideas**

- Destiny is a natural force that cannot be changed ("Destiny flows according to its own rules").
- Success cannot always be obtained, but it is the duty of individuals to strive for it.
- The virtuous and righteous should be respected and admired (Karna's generosity and love for giving are highlighted).
- Death is inevitable, and even the bravest warriors can fall in battle.
