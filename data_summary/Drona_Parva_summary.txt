Summary of Drona parva. This is the summary of main parva, Drona parva.

**Summary**

The text describes several battles between the Pandavas and their allies against the Kauravas in the Mahabharata war. The battles are intense and chaotic, with various warriors clashing in fierce duels. Drona, a skilled warrior, is killed by Dhrishtadyumna after being overcome with grief over his son's death. Arjuna kills Jayadratha, the king of Sindhu, using divine weapons. Other key events include the death of Abhimanyu, the son of Subhadra and Arjuna, and the killing of Ghatotkacha by Karna.

The text also describes various philosophical and moral ideas, including the importance of following one's dharma (duty), the consequences of breaking one's vows or promises, and the concept of destiny and its role in shaping events. The text highlights the bravery and skill of individual warriors but also their vulnerability to injury and death.

**Key events and characters**

- Drona is killed by Dhrishtadyumna
- Arjuna kills Jayadratha using divine weapons
- Abhimanyu, the son of Subhadra and Arjuna, dies in battle
- Ghatotkacha is killed by Karna
- Bhima fights against Alayudha and his followers
- Satyaki fights against Duryodhana, his childhood friend
- Rishis come to Drona and tell him it's time for him to die

**Philosophical or Moral Ideas**

- The importance of following one's dharma (duty)
- The consequences of breaking one's vows or promises
- The concept of destiny and its role in shaping events
- The idea that even the most powerful warriors can fall in battle
