Summary of Mriga Svapna Bhaya parva (under Aranyaka parva)

**Summary**:
Janamejaya asked Vaishampayana about what the Pandavas did after freeing Duryodhana. Vaishampayana told Janamejaya that one night, Yudhishthira had a dream where some deer appeared to him, trembling and saluting him. The deer begged Yudhishthira to find another place to live because his brothers' hunting was reducing their numbers. Moved by compassion, Yudhishthira promised to help the deer and told his brothers that they should move to a new forest called Kamyaka.

**Key events and characters**
- Janamejaya asked Vaishampayana about the Pandavas after freeing Duryodhana.
- Yudhishthira had a dream where some deer appeared to him, begging for help.
- The deer told Yudhishthira that his brothers' hunting was reducing their numbers and they needed a new place to live.
- Yudhishthira promised to help the deer and moved with his family and servants to the forest of Kamyaka.

**Philosophical or Moral Ideas**
- Compassion towards all beings is an important aspect of being a good king (as shown by Yudhishthira's actions).
- The importance of considering the welfare of others, even animals, in one's decisions.
