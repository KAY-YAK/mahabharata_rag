Summary of Sanjaya Yana parva (under Udyoga parva)

**Summary**

Dhritarashtra instructs Sanjaya to visit Upaplavya and show homage to Ajatashatru (Yudhishthira) and tell him that it is fortunate that they have stationed themselves in the village. Dhritarashtra asks Sanjaya to honour them with words of conciliation, saying that they did not deserve the difficulties of dwelling in exile and that they will soon be at peace with the Kurus.

Sanjaya meets Yudhishthira and greets him, then reports back to Dhritarashtra about their conversation. Dhritarashtra is concerned about war and asks Sanjaya to persuade the Pandavas to make peace. Sanjaya returns to Hastinapura and meets with Dhritarashtra again, reporting on his conversation with Yudhishthira.

**Key events and characters**

- Dhritarashtra instructs Sanjaya to visit Upaplavya
- Sanjaya meets Yudhishthira and greets him
- Dhritarashtra asks Sanjaya to persuade the Pandavas to make peace
- Sanjaya returns to Hastinapura and meets with Dhritarashtra again
- Dhritarashtra is concerned about war
- Yudhishthira reports back to Dhritarashtra on his conversation with Sanjaya

**Philosophical or Moral Ideas**

- The importance of dharma (righteous conduct) in achieving success and happiness
- The dangers of desire and the need for self-control
- The importance of following one's duty and performing good deeds
- The idea that a person's actions have consequences in this life and the next
- The concept of karma (action, the fruits of action, and consequent destiny)
- The idea that a king should protect his subjects and act in accordance with dharma.
