Summary of Jatasura vadha parva (under Aranyaka parva)

**Summary**

Vaishampayana tells a story about the Pandavas living on a mountain with their wife Droupadi and her sons. A rakshasa named Jatasura disguises himself as a brahmana and serves them, but eventually abducts Yudhishthira, Nakula, and Droupadi while Bhimasena is away hunting. The Pandavas are rescued by Bhima, who engages in a fierce battle with Jatasura using trees, rocks, and fists. In the end, Bhima kills Jatasura by detaching his head from his trunk.

**Key events and characters**

- A rakshasa named Jatasura disguises himself as a brahmana and serves the Pandavas.
- Jatasura abducts Yudhishthira, Nakula, and Droupadi while Bhimasena is away hunting.
- Bhima rescues his brothers and Droupadi from Jatasura's clutches.
- Bhima engages in a fierce battle with Jatasura using trees, rocks, and fists.
- Bhima kills Jatasura by detaching his head from his trunk.

**Philosophical or Moral Ideas**

- Yudhishthira tells Jatasura that harming a king is wrong unless there has been an act of injury.
- Sahadeva says that a kshatriya's greatest merit is to fall in battle and give up their life, or to triumph over an enemy.
- Bhima warns Jatasura that he will not be able to escape his fate and that he will meet the same end as other rakshasas who have been killed by Bhima.
