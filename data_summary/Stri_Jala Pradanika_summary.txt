Summary of Jala Pradanika parva (under Stri parva)

**Summary**

The text describes the scene where the Pandavas and their relatives gather at the Ganga river to perform rituals for their loved ones who died in the war. Kunti, the mother of the Pandavas, speaks about her eldest son Karna, who was a brave warrior but considered an outcast because he was born from a god to a lower-caste woman. She tells the Pandavas that they should have considered him their brother and performed rituals for him. The Pandavas are overcome with grief and perform the water-rites for Karna, along with his wives.

**Key events and characters**

- The Pandavas and their relatives gather at the Ganga river to perform rituals.
- Kunti speaks about her eldest son Karna and tells the Pandavas to consider him their brother.
- The Pandavas are overcome with grief and perform the water-rites for Karna.
- Yudhishthira, the eldest Pandava, laments about Karna's death and its impact on the family.
- Karna's wives are brought to the Ganga river to participate in the rituals.

**Philosophical or Moral Ideas**

- The importance of considering all members of a family as one's own, regardless of their birth or social status.
- The value of performing rituals for loved ones who have passed away.
- The idea that keeping secrets can lead to harm and suffering.
