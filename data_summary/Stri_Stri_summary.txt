Summary of Stri parva (under Stri parva)

**Summary**

Janamejaya asks Vaishampayana about King Dhritarashtra's actions after Vyasa departed. Vaishampayana tells Janamejaya that Dhritarashtra summoned Gandhari, the women of the Bharata lineage, and other women to his presence. They were overcome with grief upon seeing their husbands and sons dead on the battlefield. The king and his companions then left the city, accompanied by thousands of lamenting women.

Dharmaraja Yudhishthira and his brothers went to meet Dhritarashtra, who was overcome with sorrow. Krishna comforted him, telling him that he had acted in accordance with dharma, but had lost control due to his affection for Duryodhana. Dhritarashtra then saw the destruction of the Kurus and was overcome with grief.

Gandhari, seeing her sons and grandsons dead on the battlefield, fell down on the ground and lamented piteously. She spoke to Krishna about the destruction of the Kurus and how it had been inevitable. She also saw Duryodhana lying dead on the ground and lamented his death.

The women of the Bharata lineage were seen running around, shrieking and lamenting their husbands and sons. They were overcome with grief and did not recognize their loved ones due to the destruction of the battlefield. Gandhari saw her son Vikarna, who was revered as wise, lying dead on the ground. She also saw Karna's wife, Vrishasena's mother, who had fallen down senseless upon seeing Karna's body.

Gandhari then spoke about the death of various warriors, including Jayadratha, Shalya, Bhagadatta, and Drona. She lamented their deaths and how they were brought down by the Pandavas in battle. The women from different kingdoms and regions came to see their husbands and sons lying dead on the battlefield.

- Janamejaya asks Vaishampayana about King Dhritarashtra's actions after Vyasa departed.
- Dhritarashtra summons Gandhari, the women of the Bharata lineage, and other women to his presence.
- The king and his companions leave the city, accompanied by thousands of lamenting women.

- Various warriors die in battle, including Vikarna, Karna's wife, Jayadratha, Shalya, Bhagadatta, and Drona.

**Philosophical or Moral Ideas**

- The inevitability of fate: Gandhari says that the destruction of the Kurus was inevitable and that one should not sorrow over something that has already occurred.
- The importance of dharma: Krishna tells Dhritarashtra that he had acted in accordance with dharma, but lost control due to his affection for Duryodhana.
- The consequences of one's actions: Gandhari curses Krishna, saying that he will slay his own relatives and suffer a horrible death.
