Summary of Vairata parva (under Virata parva)

**Summary**:
The Pandavas, led by Yudhishthira, are forced to live in disguise in the city of Matsya (Virata) after being exiled from their kingdom. To maintain their cover, they take on different roles and names: Yudhishthira becomes a brahmana named Kanka, skilled in dice; Bhima is appointed as the chief chef, Ballava; Arjuna disguises himself as a eunuch, Brihannada; Nakula becomes a charioteer, Granthika; Sahadeva tends to the cows, Tantipala; and Droupadi becomes a sairandhri (maidservant), Malini. They live in Matsya for a year, performing various tasks and duties while maintaining their true identities hidden.

**Key events and characters**
- Yudhishthira asks about his ancestors' lives in disguise in the city of Virata.
- Vaishampayana recounts how the Pandavas lived in disguise in Matsya (Virata).
- The Pandavas take on different roles: Yudhishthira as Kanka, Bhima as Ballava, Arjuna as Brihannada, Nakula as Granthika, Sahadeva as Tantipala, and Droupadi as Malini.
- They live in Matsya for a year, performing various tasks and duties while maintaining their true identities hidden.
- Bhima fights with the giant wrestler Jimuta and emerges victorious.
- The Pandavas perform various duties for King Virata, including fighting with tigers, lions, and elephants.

**Philosophical or Moral Ideas**
- The importance of living in disguise to maintain one's safety and dignity (Dhoumya).
- The need to be cautious and adaptable when living in a king's household.
- The value of good conduct and behavior when serving a king.
- The danger of being too proud or arrogant when serving a king.
- The importance of honesty, self-control, and loyalty when living in disguise.
