Summary of Harana Harika parva (under Adi parva)

**Summary**

The text describes the events following Arjuna's abduction of Subhadra, Krishna's sister. Vasudeva, Krishna's father, advises that Arjuna return and marry Subhadra to avoid dishonor to their lineage. Arjuna returns and marries Subhadra, but Droupadi is jealous and tries to persuade him to leave her for Subhadra. However, Arjuna pacifies Droupadi and eventually visits Subhadra, who is dressed in cowherd attire. The Vrishnis arrive at Indraprastha with gifts, including chariots, cows, horses, mules, women, gold, and elephants. The Pandavas spend time in pleasure with the Vrishnis before returning to Dvaravati.

**Key events and characters**

- Arjuna abducts Subhadra
- Vasudeva advises Arjuna to return and marry Subhadra
- Arjuna returns and marries Subhadra
- Droupadi is jealous of Subhadra
- The Vrishnis arrive at Indraprastha with gifts
- The Pandavas spend time in pleasure with the Vrishnis
- Soubhadra, Abhimanyu, and five sons of Droupadi are born

**Philosophical or Moral Ideas**

- Vasudeva advises that appeasement is better than defeat
- The importance of family ties and alliances
- The value of gifts and hospitality in building relationships
