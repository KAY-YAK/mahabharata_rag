Summary of Vaivahika parva (under Virata parva)

**Summary**

The story revolves around King Virata of Matsya, who had allied himself with the Pandavas during their exile. After defeating the Trigartas in battle, Virata's son Uttara returns home a hero. However, upon his return, he is met with a surprise - Arjuna, one of the Pandava brothers, has been living in disguise as a cook in Virata's palace. When Arjuna reveals himself and his brothers to Virata, they are welcomed back with open arms. The king offers them his entire kingdom and asks for their forgiveness for past mistakes. An alliance is formed between the Matsyas and the Pandavas, and their marriage takes place with great festivities.

**Key events and characters**

- King Virata of Matsya forms an alliance with the Pandavas during their exile.
- Uttara returns home a hero after defeating the Kurus in battle.
- Arjuna reveals himself to Virata as one of the Pandava brothers living in disguise.
- The Pandavas are welcomed back by Virata and his kingdom.
- An alliance is formed between the Matsyas and the Pandavas.
- Marriage takes place between Abhimanyu (Arjuna's son) and Uttara, with great festivities.

**Philosophical or Moral Ideas**

- The importance of forgiveness and seeking pardon for past mistakes.
- The value of self-control and restraint in speech and action.
- The concept of dharma (duty) and its importance in maintaining social order.
- The idea that true greatness lies not in wealth or power, but in character and virtue.
