Summary of Samshaptaka Vadha parva (under Drona parva)

**Summary**

The text describes a fierce battle between two armies led by King Suyodhana (Duryodhana) and Yudhishthira. Sanjaya tells King Dhritarashtra about the preparations for the battle, where Drona's army is arranged in a formation called "vyuha," resembling a Garuda bird. The battle becomes violent, with warriors fighting each other with various weapons, including arrows, javelins, swords, and battleaxes. Elephants are also used in the battle, causing destruction and chaos.

Dhrishtadyumna attacks Drona, but Durmukha counters him, leading to a fierce battle between the two. The warriors fight each other with no regard for life or death, making it difficult to distinguish friend from foe. Many elephants are killed or wounded, and their riders are either slain or flee.

The text describes the scene as a tumultuous and fearful battle, where warriors fight each other with showers of arrows, causing destruction and chaos. The earth is covered with the bodies of men, horses, chariots, and elephants, creating a slush of blood and flesh.

Drona's army surrounds Yudhishthira, but he receives them with a mighty shower of arrows. Satyajit attacks Drona, but he is pierced by ten sharp arrows. Vrika then attacks Drona, piercing him between the breasts with sixty arrows. Drona kills Vrika and his charioteer and horses with six arrows.

The battle continues, with many warriors being killed or wounded on both sides. The text describes the scene as a terrible and fearful battle, where warriors fight each other with no regard for life or death.
