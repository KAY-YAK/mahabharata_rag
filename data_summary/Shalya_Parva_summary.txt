Summary of Shalya parva. This is the summary of main parva, Shalya parva.

**Summary**

The text describes various battles between the Pandavas and their enemies, particularly Duryodhana's followers. The great warrior Shalya fought against Bhima but was eventually struck down by Arjuna. Many soldiers were killed or wounded in the fierce battle. Duryodhana led his army against the Pandavas but ultimately retreated after seeing his army being destroyed. He later emerged from a lake and informed Sanjaya to tell Dhritarashtra about his defeat and death.

The remaining Kourava soldiers, except for three rathas (Ashvatthama, Kripa, and Kritavarma), were killed by the Pandavas. Sanjaya was released alive by Dhrishtadyumna and returned to Hastinapura. Yuyutsu entered the city with the king's wives.

The text also describes various stories from Hindu mythology, including the consecration of Kartikeya as the lord of a sacred place, Balarama's visit to various tirthas, and the birth of Sarasvata. The final story is about Kuni-Gargya generating a daughter through his mind powers.

In another scene, Duryodhana's son Ashvatthama vows to take revenge on the Panchalas for Drona's death after seeing his father's condition.

**Key events and characters**

- Bhima fights against Shalya in a fierce battle.
- Duryodhana leads the charge against the Pandavas but retreats after seeing his army being destroyed.
- Many soldiers are killed or wounded in the battle, including Chekitana and Uluka.
- The remaining Kourava soldiers, except for Ashvatthama, Kripa, and Kritavarma, are killed by the Pandavas.
- Sanjaya is released alive by Dhrishtadyumna and returns to Hastinapura.
- Yuyutsu enters the city with the king's wives.
- Kartikeya becomes the general of the gods' army and grants a boon to the gods that he will kill their enemies.
- Balarama visits various tirthas, including Badarapachana, Ramatirtha, Yamunatirtha, Adityatirtha, and Sarasvata.
- Asita-Devala tries to follow Jaigishavya's path but is unable to do so due to his own limitations.
- Ashvatthama vows to take revenge on the Panchalas for Drona's death.

**Philosophical or Moral Ideas**

- The importance of strength and bravery in protecting one's people and territory.
- The power of divine intervention in times of crisis.
- The idea that even the most powerful enemies can be defeated through determination and courage.
- Good fortune and guidance from a higher power (Krishna) can lead to eternal worlds.
