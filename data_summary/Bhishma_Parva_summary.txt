Summary of Bhishma parva. This is the summary of main parva, Bhishma parva.

**Summary**

The text describes various events related to the Mahabharata war, including the preparation for battle by the Pandavas, Somakas, and Kouravas, and the description of the geography of Jambudvipa and its surrounding dvipas. It also discusses yoga and its practice as described by Lord Krishna to Arjuna, emphasizing the importance of self-control and detachment from desires. Additionally, it describes a great battle fought by Bhishma against the Pandavas on the tenth day, where Bhishma is severely wounded and falls from his chariot.

**Key events and characters**

- Janamejaya asks Vaishampayana about the Kurus' battle.
- Dhritarashtra asks Sanjaya to describe Jambudvipa and its surrounding dvipas.
- Lord Krishna tells Arjuna about the importance of yoga.
- Bhishma fights against the Pandavas on the tenth day, using his supreme weapons.
- Arjuna severely wounds Bhishma with arrows.
- The swans sent by Ganga try to persuade Bhishma to leave during dakshinayana.
- Duryodhana tries to comfort his father after Bhishma's downfall.

**Philosophical or Moral Ideas**

- The importance of following dharma during war.
- Yoga is performing actions without attachment to their outcomes.
- A yogi should control their mind and senses, be tranquil, and focus on their atman (self).
- Self-control is essential for achieving yoga.
- Detachment from desires is necessary for yoga practice.
- Dharma (righteousness) is present in Bhishma's soul.
- Bhishma's decision to wait until uttarayana before giving up his life shows his commitment to dharma.
- Arjuna's ability to provide water for Bhishma using his parjanya weapon demonstrates his mastery over the elements.
