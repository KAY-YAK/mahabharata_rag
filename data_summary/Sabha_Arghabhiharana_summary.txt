Summary of Arghabhiharana parva (under Sabha parva)

**Summary**

The story is about a great sacrifice performed by King Yudhishthira. Many brahmanas and kings attend the ceremony, including Narada, a wise sage. The assembly discusses and argues about various aspects of dharma (righteousness) and artha (wealth). Shishupala, a king from Chedi, is unhappy with the homage shown to Krishna, considering it an insult to other kings. Bhishma explains that Krishna deserves the homage due to his strength, valour, and knowledge. The assembly agrees, and Sahadeva declares that anyone who cannot accept this will be considered inferior.

**Key events and characters**

- Vaishampayana describes a great sacrifice performed by King Yudhishthira.
- Many brahmanas and kings attend the ceremony, including Narada.
- Shishupala is unhappy with the homage shown to Krishna.
- Bhishma explains that Krishna deserves the homage due to his strength, valour, and knowledge.
- Sahadeva declares that anyone who cannot accept this will be considered inferior.
- A shower of flowers rains down on Sahadeva's head in approval.

**Philosophical or Moral Ideas**

- The importance of dharma (righteousness) and artha (wealth).
- The value of knowledge, strength, and valour in a person.
- The idea that one should accept the homage shown to someone who deserves it.
- The concept of "honor" and how it is given to those who deserve it.
