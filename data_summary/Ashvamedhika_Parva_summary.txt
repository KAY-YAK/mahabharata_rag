Summary of Ashvamedhika parva. This is the summary of main parva, Ashvamedhika parva.

**Summary**

Vaishampayana tells the story of King Yudhishthira performing a great horse sacrifice, which is advised by Vyasa. The king becomes energetic like Shakra after the successful sacrifice. However, a mongoose appears and criticizes the king's sacrifice, telling the story of a brahmana who obtained heaven with his family through donations. Vaishampayana explains that many kings have performed sacrifices to achieve fame and go to heaven, but also tells the story of Shakra's criticized sacrifice. The mongoose is revealed to be Krodha, who was cursed for speaking ill of dharma.

**Key events and characters**

- King Yudhishthira performs a great horse sacrifice.
- Vyasa advises Yudhishthira to conduct the sacrifice with knowledgeable officiating priests.
- A mongoose appears at the sacrifice and speaks in a human voice.
- The mongoose tells the story of a brahmana who lived by the vow of unchha and donated only a trifle.
- Vaishampayana explains that many kings have performed sacrifices and obtained fame in this world and gone to heaven.
- Shakra's sacrifice is criticized by the maharshis for performing a sacrifice with slaughter of animals.
- Krodha is revealed to be the mongoose who censured Dharma's son Yudhishthira.

**Philosophical or Moral Ideas**

- The importance of following sacred texts and rituals in Hinduism.
- The concept of dharma (righteousness) and its importance in achieving success and going to heaven.
- The idea that sacrifices are not equal to donations made with faith and purity.
