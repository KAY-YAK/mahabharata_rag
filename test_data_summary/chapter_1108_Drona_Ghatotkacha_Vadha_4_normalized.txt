The two engage in a fierce duel, with Ghatotkacha using his magical powers to create illusions and mayhem. However, Ashvatthama proves to be a formidable opponent, using his arrows to counter Ghatotkacha's attacks.

As the battle rages on, Drona's son begins to gain the upper hand, killing many of Ghatotkacha's rakshasa followers and eventually striking down Bhima's son himself. The passage describes the chaos and destruction caused by the battle, with bodies strewn about and the earth covered in blood.
